PART I

Problem Context and Objectives

3

Problem Statement and Clinical Background

Pancreatic cancer remains one of the most lethal malignancies. Preoperative risk stratification helps surgical teams, patients, and families plan treatment intensity and supportive care.

  • 1Low overall survival compared with many other cancers
  • 2Routine preoperative labs and tumor markers collected before surgery
  • 3Can these features predict 1-year survival and reveal patient subgroups?
Research Question

Can routine preoperative blood tests and tumor markers predict 1-year survival in pancreatic cancer patients — and do the same features define clinically meaningful subgroups?

Speaker notes: Emphasize clinical motivation. Do not overclaim deployment.
4

Project Objectives

1
Supervised Learning

Train a tuned Random Forest to classify 1-year survival (survived >=1 yr vs died <1 yr) from 20 preoperative features. Report accuracy, macro F1, AUC-ROC, and per-class recall.

2
Unsupervised Learning

Apply K-Means (k=3) on scaled features to identify preoperative patient phenotypes. Profile clusters clinically and test survival association post hoc (chi-square).

3
Synthesis

Compare Gini feature importance with cluster-differentiating features. Bridge RF predicted probabilities across clusters to assess directional alignment.

4
Reproducible Pipeline

Document every preprocessing choice and deliberate non-action (no SMOTE, no outlier removal, no transform). Use random_state=42 for split, tuning, and clustering reproducibility.

PART VII

Limitations, Future Work, Conclusion

27

Limitations

Data

  1. Moderate class imbalance (2.2:1) despite class_weight
  2. Weak univariate correlations (all |r| < 0.15)
  3. Retrospective single-source; no external validation
  4. Missing staging, surgery, treatment variables
  5. Pre-standardized labs — raw units not in file

Model & Analysis

  1. RF overfit reduced but present (85.8% train vs 61.9% test)
  2. Class-0 recall 36.4% (20/55) - improved vs legacy tuning but still insufficient alone
  3. K-Means assumes spherical, equal-variance clusters
  4. Multicollinearity may affect ratio-feature interpretation
  5. Cluster-survival association not significant (p=0.2546)
  6. PCA 2D captures only 30.8% of variance
Speaker notes: Show scientific maturity. Invite questions.
28

Future Work

1
Model Benchmarking

Compare XGBoost/SVM; deploy best model with SHAP explanations.

2
Alternative Clustering

Evaluate DBSCAN and hierarchical methods with survival validation.

3
Feature Transformations

Apply Yeo-Johnson before K-Means to reduce skewness effects.

Use RFE or LASSO for a minimal interpretable predictor set.

Test models on an independent hospital cohort.

Richer Clinical Data

Add TNM staging, resectability, and treatment variables.

Survival Analysis

Extend to Cox proportional hazards and Kaplan-Meier curves.

Resampling Methods

Nested Cross-Validation

Obtain unbiased hyperparameter tuning estimates.
